In 2025, access to digital technology continues to expand through government-supported initiatives designed to bridge the digital divide. Programs now offer free government devices such as tablets, smartphones, and even iPhones to eligible households, ensuring that families can stay connected to educational resources, job opportunities, and essential services. The goal of these initiatives is to promote equal access to technology, especially for students, job seekers, and seniors who may otherwise be left behind in a digital-first world. Many of these programs include not only the devices themselves but also affordable internet access plans, training on how to use the devices, and support for troubleshooting common issues.

Smartphones, including select iPhone models, are also available in certain states as part of expanded communication assistance. These devices make it easier for people to stay in touch with loved ones, apply for jobs, or access telehealth appointments. The free devices are typically provided through partnerships between the government, telecommunications companies, and certified providers.
